Punjabi Chole

About the Dish:

A chickpea dish that is cooked in a spicy gravy. It gets its authentic and unique taste from the addition of various spices like cumin, bayleaf, cinnamon, chana masala, garam masala and turmeric.It is best enjoyed with puri or bhatura. Its appeal increases when it is served with cut onions, chutneys, pickles etc. A dish which is rich in protein makes it a perfect food for you and your child's lunchbox.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of White Chickpeas
  • 1 medium sized Onion
  • 1 medium sized Tomato
  • 2-3 Green Chillies
  • 1 inch Ginger
  • 1 teaspoon Jeera (Cumin Seeds)
  • 2 Cloves
  • 2 inch Cinnamon stick
  • 1 Black Cardamom
  • 3-4 Black Peppercorns
  • 1 Bayleaf
  • 1/4 teaspoon Asafoetida
  • 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric Powder
  • 1 teaspoon Red Chilli Powder
  • 2 teaspoons Chana Masala Powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon Garam Masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon Dry Mango Powder
  • Salt as per taste
  • 2 tablespoons Oil

Directions:

  1. Soak the white chickpeas for a minimum 7-8 hours. After soaking them, wash and rinse the same.
  2. Heat oil in a pressure cooker. Add cumin seeds and asafoetida with cloves, cinnamon stick, black cardamom, black peppercorn and bay leaf.
  3. Then add grated ginger, chopped green chilies and finely chopped onion.
  4. Saute till the onions turn golden brown or translucent.
  5. Then add chana masala, garam masala, turmeric powder, red chilli powder, dry mango powder and salt. If you do not have dry mango powder, you can replace it with 1 teaspoon Jaljeera or 2 teaspoons tamarind paste or even lemon juice.
  6. Cook for a couple of minutes to roast all the spices.
  7. Add finely chopped tomato to the tempering.
  8. Saute till the tomatoes are cooked and the gravy starts to leave oil on the sides.
  9. Add the chickpeas to the cooker along with 3 cups of water and cook on high heat for one whistle. Then turn the flame to low and let them cook for around 30 minutes.
  10. Open the pressure cooker after all the steam has been released to check the consistency. The consistency should not be too thin nor too dry. Simmer for 4-5 minutes to thicken the gravy if it is too thin. Or add a little water if dry to adjust the consistency.
  11. Garnish with coriander leaves, chopped or sliced onions,tomatoes and lime wedges.
  12. Serve them hot with bhatura or poori.
