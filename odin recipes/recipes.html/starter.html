Paneer Chilly

About the dish:

Paneer Chilly is one of the most popular dishes in the Indo-Chinese cuisine. It is made by coating the paneer with flour or batter and frying them.The Paneer cubes then are tossed in a spicy, salty, tangy, sweet sauce along with a lot of seasonings of vegetables like garlic, bell peppers and ginger. It is an excellent accompaniment to Fried Rice or Noodles. This is a dish that tastes heavenly and is perfect for beating the cold weather.

Ingredients:

    For the Paneer coating

  • 1 teaspoon Refined Wheat Flour
  • 1.5 teaspoon Cornflour
  • 1/4 teaspoon Pepper Powder
  • 2 teaspoon Water
  • 200 gms Paneer
  • Oil to fry

    • For the Gravy

  • 2 tablespoons of Oil
  • 1 tablespoon of Chopped Chillies
  • 4 pieces of Green Chillies
  • 1.5 teaspoon of Chopped Garlic
  • 1 teaspoon Chopped Ginger
  • 2 tablespoons of Onions finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of Spring Onions finely chopped
  • 10-15 one inch of Spring Onions strings
  • 10-15 cubes of Capsicum
  • Salt as per taste
  • 1 teaspoon of Dark Soya Sauce
  • 1.5 teaspoon Green Chilli Sauce
  • 1.5 teaspoon Chilli paste/Schezwan Paste
  • 1 teaspoon Tomato Sauce
  • 1 teaspoon White Pepper Powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon Pepper Powder
  • 1 teaspoon Garam masala
  • 300 ml Water
  • 1 tablespoon Cornflour
  • 2 pinches of Sugar

Directions:

  1. Add Refined flour, Corn Flour, Salt and Pepper Powder and mix well. Coat the Paneer cubes with the made mixture.
  2. Add some water and mix with a spoon or rotate the vessel until the cubes are well coated with the flour.
  3. Fry the Paneer cubes on medium flame until they turn light golden and the flour coating turns crisp. Set aside.
  4. Heat oil in a pan and add Ginger Garlic paste, Green Chillies and Onion.Fry until the onions turn soft.
  5. Now add Onions, Capsicum strings, Green Chilli pieces and cook on high flame for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Then add the remaining sauces, powders and fry on high flame until the Oil separates.
  7. When the oil separates, add 300 ml water and boil it on high flame. After 2-3 minutes, add the Paneer pieces and cook for 2 minutes.
  8. In the simmering Gravy, add 1 tablespoon of Corn Flour mixed in 3 tablespoons of water and let it boil for 2 minutes on high flame until it turns thick.
  9. When the Gravy thickens, add the inch-long Spring Onion pieces and let it boil for another minute. Then sprinkle the chopped Spring Onions.Switch off the gas and serve hot.
